The porcino is a very good protein carrier. It is very similar to the best animal proteins and particularly easy to digest. It is also rich in free amino acids.
Most of the fatty acids contained (84.5%) are unsaturated, around half of them are polyunsaturated. These include linoleic acid with 42.2 percent of all fatty acids and linolenic acid (0.2%). The monounsaturated fatty acids it contains include oleic acid (36.1% of all fatty acids); The saturated fatty acids include palmitic (9.8%) and stearic acid (2.7%).
100 grams of meat contain an average of 500 milligrams of ergosterol, mainly in the hat and hymenophore, and 30 milligrams of ergosterol peroxide. The latter has anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects, among other things.

**Identification:**
Smell: tasty, mushroom
Taste: mild, tasty mushroom, also raw eddible
Cap: convex, 4-20cm, wildleather brown, hazelnut brown, rolled on mostly white edge
Hymenium: adnate, pores, **Weißlich, gelblich, gelbgrün, später olivegrün, vereinzelt auf Druck auch bläulich bist grünlich verfärbend.**
Stipe: bare, **Weißlich, bräunlich überfasert. Der Stiel besitzt ein weißes, im Alter bräunliches Netz!**
Flesh: **Weißlich, nicht verfärbend, unter der Huthaut kurze rotbräunliche Zone nicht wie beim** [**Sommersteinpilz**](https://www.123pilzsuche.de/daten/details/Sommersteinpilz2004.htm) **weiß. Auch der** [**Kiefernsteinpilz**](https://www.123pilzsuche.de/daten/details/Kiefernsteinpilz.htm) **hat diese rötliche Verfärbung unter der Huthaut.**
Veil:
Spores/microscopic features: **Olivbraun (12,4-19,2 x 4,5-5,5 Pleurozystiden + Cheilozystiden 35-70 x 5,5-10 µm, spindelig schlank bis flaschenförmig).**
Growing Characteristic:
Natural habitat: **Mischwald, gern Fichten, Tannen, Kiefern, aber auch bei Eichen, Hainbuchen, Linden, Kastanien, Buchen, Birken und anderen Laubbäumen, Symbiosepilz, Frühsommer bis Spätherbst.**

Nutritional content of 100g:
*Boletus edulis* mushrooms are 9% [carbohydrates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbohydrate" \o "Carbohydrate), 3% [fat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fat" \o "Fat), and 7% [protein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein" \o "Protein) (table). Fresh mushrooms consist of over 80% moisture,[\[125\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boletus_edulis" \l "cite_note-Ouzouni2006-126) although reported values tend to differ somewhat as moisture content can be affected by environmental temperature and [relative humidity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relative_humidity" \o "Relative humidity) during growth and storage.[\[126\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boletus_edulis" \l "cite_note-127) The carbohydrate component contains the [monosaccharides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monosaccharide" \o "Monosaccharide) [glucose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glucose" \o "Glucose), [mannitol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mannitol" \o "Mannitol) and α,α-[trehalose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trehalose" \o "Trehalose), the [polysaccharide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polysaccharide" \o "Polysaccharide) [glycogen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glycogen" \o "Glycogen), and the water-insoluble structural polysaccharide [chitin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chitin" \o "Chitin), which accounts for up to 80–90% of dry matter in mushroom [cell walls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_wall" \o "Cell wall). Chitin, [hemicellulose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemicellulose" \o "Hemicellulose), and [pectin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pectin" \o "Pectin)\-like carbohydrates—all indigestible by humans—contribute to high proportion of insoluble fibre in *B. edulis*.[\[127\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boletus_edulis" \l "cite_note-Kalac2009-128)
The total [lipid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lipid" \o "Lipid), or crude fat, content makes up 3% of the dry matter of the mushroom. The proportion of [fatty acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatty_acid" \o "Fatty acid) (expressed as a % of total fatty acids) are: [palmitic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palmitic_acid" \o "Palmitic acid), 10%; [stearic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stearic_acid" \o "Stearic acid), 3%; [oleic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oleic_acid" \o "Oleic acid), 36%; and [linoleic acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linoleic_acid" \o "Linoleic acid), 42%.[\[128\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boletus_edulis" \l "cite_note-129)

Medicinical Properties:
A comparative study of the [amino acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acid) composition of eleven Portuguese wild edible mushroom species showed *Boletus edulis* to have the highest total amino acid content.[\[129\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boletus_edulis#cite_note-Ribeiro2008b-130)[\[130\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boletus_edulis#cite_note-131)
*B. edulis* mushrooms are rich in the [dietary minerals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mineral_\(nutrient\)), [sodium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodium), [iron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron), [calcium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calcium), and [magnesium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnesium), with amounts varying according to the mushroom component and to soil composition in the geographic region of [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China) where they were sampled.[\[127\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boletus_edulis#cite_note-Kalac2009-128)[\[131\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boletus_edulis#cite_note-Su-132) They also have high content of [B vitamins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B_vitamins) and [tocopherols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tocopherol).[\[132\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boletus_edulis#cite_note-Jaworska-133) *B. edulis* contains appreciable amounts of [selenium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selenium), a [trace mineral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trace_mineral),[\[133\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boletus_edulis#cite_note-134) although the [bioavailability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioavailability) of mushroom-derived selenium is low.
