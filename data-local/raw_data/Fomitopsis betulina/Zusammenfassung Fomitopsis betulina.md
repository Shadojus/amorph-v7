FOMITOPSIS BETULINA (SYN. PIPTOPORUS BETULINUS, BOLETUS BETULINUS)
The „man in the ice“, commonly known as Ötzi, a roughly 5,300-year-old glacial mummy, carried two birch porlings with him.
In earlier times, the fungus was primarily important as a medicine. The body of the fruit, cut into thin strips, was used as a bandage for wound healing, among other things for its anti-inflammatory ingredients. It is very good for stomach ailments of all kinds when used as a tea infusion.
Studies confirmed the high antioxidant effect and could, as well as antibacterial and antiviral effects (especially with influenza strains).
In naturopathy, it is said to be effective against tumor diseases in various ways by reducing the side effects of chemotherapy. It has anti-inflammatory, antibiotic, and antiviral effects. It also helps against worm disease and other intestinal parasites. Promotes hair growth and improves complexion.

Medicinical Properties:
**Schon der „Ötzi“(Gletschermumie) hatte einen Birkenporling bei sich. Er ist sehr gut gegen Magenbeschwerden aller Art, wenn er als Teesud verwendet wird. Unsere Erfahrungen bestätigten dies bereits mehrfach! In der Naturheilkunde soll er gut gegen Tumorerkrankungen verschiedenster Formen helfen, indem er Nebenwirkungen der Chemotherapie mindert. In Russland, Tschechien und anderen Ländern wurde er schon lange gegen Tumorerkrankung des Magen und des Darms verwendet. Er wirkt entzündungshemmend, antibiotisch und antiviral. Außerdem hilft er gegen Wurmerkrankung und anderen Darmparasiten. Er fördert das Haarwachstum und verbessert das Hautbild.**
*Fomitopsis betulina* has been widely used in [traditional medicines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_medicine), and has been extensively researched for its [phytochemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phytochemistry) and [pharmacological](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharmacology) activity.[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fomitopsis_betulina#cite_note-Guevara-Gonzalez_2014-20) Phytochemicals include [phenolic acids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phenolic_acid), [indole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indole) compounds, [sterols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sterol), and [triterpenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triterpene).[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fomitopsis_betulina#cite_note-21)
[Agaric acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agaric_acid) found in the fruit body of the fungus, is poisonous to the parasitic [whipworm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whipworm) *Trichuris trichura*.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fomitopsis_betulina#cite_note-pmid9851424-22) The fungus was carried by "[Ötzi the Iceman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%96tzi_the_Iceman" \o "Ötzi the Iceman)" – the 5,300 year old [mummy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mummy) found in [Tyrol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_Tyrol), with speculation that the fungus may have been used as a [laxative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laxative) to expel whipworm.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fomitopsis_betulina#cite_note-pmid9851424-22)
